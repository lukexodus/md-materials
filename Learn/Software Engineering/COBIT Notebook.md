# Governance Distinct from Management

## Core Distinction

| | **Governance** | **Management** |
|---|---|---|
| **Question asked** | Are we doing the *right* things? | Are we doing things *right*? |
| **Focus** | Direction, oversight, accountability | Execution, planning, control |
| **Who does it** | Board, senior leadership, steering committees | Managers, project leads, team leads |
| **Time horizon** | Long-term, strategic | Short-term to medium-term, operational |
| **Authority** | Sets boundaries and policies | Operates within those boundaries |

---

## Governance — Deeper Look

**What it does:**
- Sets organizational direction and strategy
- Defines policies, standards, and boundaries
- Holds management accountable for results
- Manages risk at an enterprise level
- Ensures compliance with external obligations (legal, regulatory)

**Examples:**
- A board approving an IT security policy
- An audit committee reviewing risk exposure
- A steering committee approving a project portfolio

---

## Management — Deeper Look

**What it does:**
- Plans, executes, and monitors work
- Allocates resources to meet objectives
- Solves day-to-day problems
- Reports performance upward (to governance bodies)

**Examples:**
- A project manager tracking milestones
- A team lead assigning tasks
- A department head managing a budget

---

## Simple Analogy

> **Governance** is like a **constitution** — it sets the rules of the game.
> **Management** is like a **government** — it plays the game within those rules.

---

## Why the Distinction Matters

- Confusing the two causes **accountability gaps** — e.g., when managers make strategic decisions they aren't authorized to make
- Good governance **enables** management without **replacing** it
- In audits and frameworks (CMMI, COBIT, ISO), governance and management are assessed **separately** because their responsibilities are fundamentally different

---

## In CMMI / Process Frameworks Context

- **Governance** ensures processes are defined, sponsored, and enforced at the organizational level
- **Management** ensures those processes are actually followed on projects and in teams
- Senior leadership provides governance; project/process owners provide management

---

**In short:** Governance sets the *what and why* at an organizational level. Management handles the *how and when* at an operational level. Both are necessary — but they are not interchangeable
