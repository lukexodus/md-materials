# ISACA

---

## 1. What Is ISACA?

**ISACA** stands for **Information Systems Audit and Control Association**.

- Founded: **1967**
- Headquarters: **Schaumburg, Illinois, USA**
- Type: **Non-profit professional association**
- Members: **Over 170,000 members** in more than 180 countries

Originally focused on IT auditing, ISACA has expanded into **cybersecurity, risk management, governance, and digital trust.**

---

## 2. Core Mission

ISACA's mission is to:
- Advance **digital trust**
- Support professionals in **IT audit, governance, risk, and cybersecurity**
- Develop globally recognized **frameworks, certifications, and standards**

---

## 3. ISACA Certifications

ISACA offers some of the most respected certifications in IT governance and security.

---

### 3.1 CISA – Certified Information Systems Auditor
- **Most widely known** ISACA certification
- Focus: **IT auditing, control, and assurance**
- Target: IT auditors, audit managers, consultants
- Experience required: 5 years in IS audit/control/assurance
- Exam: 150 questions, 4 hours

**Key domains:**

| Domain | Coverage |
|---|---|
| 1 | Information System Auditing Process |
| 2 | Governance & Management of IT |
| 3 | Information Systems Acquisition, Development & Implementation |
| 4 | Information Systems Operations & Business Resilience |
| 5 | Protection of Information Assets |

---

### 3.2 CISM – Certified Information Security Manager
- Focus: **Information security management**
- Target: Security managers, CISOs, IT managers
- Experience required: 5 years in information security management
- Exam: 150 questions, 4 hours

**Key domains:**

| Domain | Coverage |
|---|---|
| 1 | Information Security Governance |
| 2 | Information Security Risk Management |
| 3 | Information Security Program |
| 4 | Incident Management |

---

### 3.3 CRISC – Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control
- Focus: **Enterprise IT risk management**
- Target: Risk professionals, IT analysts, project managers
- Experience required: 3 years in risk management and IS control
- Exam: 150 questions, 4 hours

**Key domains:**

| Domain | Coverage |
|---|---|
| 1 | Governance |
| 2 | IT Risk Assessment |
| 3 | Risk Response and Reporting |
| 4 | Information Technology and Security |

---

### 3.4 CGEIT – Certified in the Governance of Enterprise IT
- Focus: **IT governance**
- Target: Senior IT executives, CIOs, governance professionals
- Experience required: 5 years in IT governance
- Considered one of the more advanced/senior certifications

---

### 3.5 CSX-P – Cybersecurity Practitioner Certification
- Focus: **Hands-on cybersecurity skills**
- Performance-based exam (not just multiple choice)
- Tests real-world ability to identify, protect, detect, respond, recover

---

### 3.6 CDPSE – Certified Data Privacy Solutions Engineer
- Focus: **Privacy by design, data privacy technology**
- Target: Engineers and developers implementing privacy solutions
- Newer certification (launched 2020)

---

### 3.7 CET – Certified in Emerging Technology
- Focus: AI, blockchain, IoT, cloud — governance and risk implications
- Newer addition to ISACA's portfolio

## 4. ISACA Frameworks

ISACA develops and maintains globally used frameworks.

---

### 4.1 COBIT – Control Objectives for Information and Related Technologies

**The flagship ISACA framework.**

- Current version: **COBIT 2019**
- Purpose: IT governance and management framework
- Used by: Enterprises, auditors, regulators worldwide

#### COBIT Core Components:
| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| **Governance System** | Principles, policies, frameworks |
| **Governance Framework** | Design factors, focus areas |
| **Goals Cascade** | Enterprise goals → IT goals → process goals |
| **Process Model** | 40 governance and management objectives |

#### COBIT 2019 – Two Core Areas:
**Governance (EDM):**

- EDM01 – Ensured Governance Framework Setting and Maintenance
- EDM02 – Ensured Benefits Delivery
- EDM03 – Ensured Risk Optimization
- EDM04 – Ensured Resource Optimization
- EDM05 – Ensured Stakeholder Engagement

**Management (PBRM – Plan, Build, Run, Monitor):**

| Domain | Focus |
|---|---|
| **APO** – Align, Plan, Organize | Strategy, architecture, risk, innovation |
| **BAI** – Build, Acquire, Implement | Projects, change, knowledge management |
| **DSS** – Deliver, Service, Support | Operations, security, incident management |
| **MEA** – Monitor, Evaluate, Assess | Performance, compliance, assurance |

#### COBIT Design Factors:
Organizations tailor COBIT based on:
- Enterprise strategy
- Risk profile
- Compliance requirements
- IT role in the organization
- Deployment model (cloud, on-prem, hybrid)

---

### 4.2 Val IT
- Framework for IT value management
- Helps organizations get maximum value from IT investments
- Now integrated into COBIT

### 4.3 Risk IT
- Framework specifically for IT risk management
- Complements COBIT
- Now largely integrated into COBIT 2019

## 5. ISACA's Relationship with Other Frameworks

| Framework | Relationship to ISACA/COBIT |
|---|---|
| **ITIL** | Operational focus; COBIT is governance-focused; often used together |
| **ISO 27001** | Security standard; aligns with CISM and COBIT DSS domain |
| **NIST CSF** | U.S. cybersecurity framework; maps to COBIT and CSX-P |
| **CMMI** | Process maturity; complements COBIT BAI domain |
| **SOX** | U.S. regulation; CISA and COBIT widely used for SOX IT compliance |
| **GDPR** | Privacy regulation; aligns with CDPSE certification |

## 8. Continuing Professional Education (CPE)

All ISACA certifications require ongoing **CPE hours** to maintain:

| Certification | Annual CPE | 3-Year Total |
|---|---|---|
| CISA | 20 hours | 120 hours |
| CISM | 20 hours | 120 hours |
| CRISC | 20 hours | 120 hours |
| CGEIT | 20 hours | 120 hours |

CPE can be earned through:
- Training courses
- Conferences
- Self-study
- Volunteering with ISACA
- Writing articles or research
